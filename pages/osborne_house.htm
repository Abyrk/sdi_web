Osborne House, York Avenue, East Cowes, Isle of Wight - PO32 6JX

Osborne House was Queen Victoria's summer residence. Queen Victoria and Prince Albert bought the Osborne estate on the Isle of Wight in 1845. There they created a private home away from court life in London and Windsor. Queen Victoria used Osborne for more than 50 years, entertaining foreign royalty and visiting ministers as well as her own extensive family, and after Prince Albert's death in 1861 she found solace there. Many of the rooms are still filled with original furniture and works of art, reflecting the couple's personal tastes, while the planting in the grounds is to Prince Albert's designs.

Not much is known of the site's earliest history. In 1705 the Blachford famile took over the estate. From 1774 to 1781 Robert Pope Blachford (1742-90) extended and adapted an existing house into a three-storey residence, with a walled kitchen garden and a brick stable block.

Information

Main entrance

The main entrance to Osborne House is on the A3021 from East Cowes. For sat navs use postcode PO32 6JT.

House and Gardens

The main house is beautiful, with many original pieces from Queen Victoria's residency. The formal terraced gardens look out on the landscaped grounds towards the private beach.

Walled kitchen gardens

Much of the fresh fruit and vegetables consumed at meal times was grown here.

Swiss cottage and gardens

This small cottage has been restored to show what life might have been like for the royal children in Victorian times.

Private beach

Queen Victoria enjoyed bathing here. To preserve her modesty she had her own changing hut that was wheeled into the sea when she was ready to swim.
