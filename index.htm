Welcome to the Isle of Wight

The Isle of Wight is an English county located in the English Channel only 4 miles off the mainland of Great Britain. Since Victorian times it has been a popular tourist destination and it has several resorts. Even Queen Victoria had a summer residence there - Osborne House. The island has a long maritime and industrial history of sail making and boat building (including the world's first hovercraft). The quickest public transport link to the mainland is the hovercraft from Southsea. The Isle has well-conserved wildlife and some of the richest cliffs and quarries for dinosaur fossils in Europe.

You can use this map to have a look round the island. Hover over it to see a satellite image and zoom in to your area of interest. You can change the size of the 'spy glass' using the up and down arrows. Interested in history? Nature? Want a family day out? There's something here for everyone.
